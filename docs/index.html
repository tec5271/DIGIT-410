Prize Pools

The prize pool for Esports tournaments is determined through a combination of factors, including sponsorship, ticket sales, and crowdfunding. Sponsorship: Many Esports tournaments are sponsored by companies that want to promote their brand and reach the large and engaged Esports audience. These sponsors can contribute significant amounts of money to the tournament's prize pool. Ticket sales: Some Esports tournaments sell tickets to fans who want to watch the event live. The revenue from ticket sales can go towards the prize pool, with a portion of each ticket sold being added to the overall prize pool. Crowdfunding: Some Esports tournaments use crowdfunding to raise money for their prize pool. This involves setting a target prize pool amount and encouraging fans to contribute through platforms like Kickstarter or GoFundMe. Fans can donate money to the prize pool in exchange for rewards, such as exclusive merchandise or the opportunity to play with the pros. In some cases, the prize pool for an Esports tournament can exceed expectations due to unexpected popularity or additional contributions from sponsors or fans. This was the case with The International, which started as a modest tournament in 2011 with a $1.6 million USD prize pool and has since grown into a massive event with a record-breaking $40 million USD prize pool in 2021. So their ability to crowdfund these events makes their large sums of money in the prize pool possible. If they were not able to rally the users of the game to support as much as they do then their funds would be much less significant. It also comes down to the developer company of the game to decide how much money they can give and still make profit off the event. Some companies in my opinion can be stingy with the allocating of prize funds.
